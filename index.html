Deconstructing the Clinical AI Trust Crisis

An operational engineering response to algorithmic black-boxes and socio-economic bias in healthcare deployment pipelines.

1. The Core Thesis

Medical professionals routinely reject machine learning models because raw risk percentages offer zero clinical context. If a model calculates a 78% readmission probability but fails to declare its underlying features, executing targeted care management is impossible. This portfolio piece resolves the deployment bottleneck by treating model explainability (SHAP) and demographic equity (4/5ths Rule Bias Auditing) as infrastructure requirements, not documentation afterthoughts.

3. Technical Architecture Overview

The system relies on a three-tier automated verification pipeline built entirely in production-grade Python and SQL:

I. Ingestion & Transformation

Simulates longitudinal Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) data profiles with pre-baked selection bias mechanisms to test auditing frameworks.

II. SHAP Attribution Matrix

Extracts local game-theoretic feature importances for individual patients, converting an uninterpretable Random Forest into explicit feature directions for clinicians.
